Executive Summary

The Global Horizon University (GHU) Alumni Connect is a web-based analytics platform designed to address the challenges of managing and analyzing data for over 2,000 alumni across various industries and graduation years.

The platform aims to improve alumni engagement, provide career services with insights into former student outcomes, and facilitate mentorship and fundraising through a full-stack web application using React, FastAPI, and MongoDB.

Problem Context & Stakeholders

Organizational Context

Global Horizon University (GHU) is a mid-sized institution with over 2,000 graduates across seven disciplines. The university faces a competitive higher education landscape, requiring strong alumni relationships for reputation, fundraising, and student placement.

Historically, career services and alumni relations offices managed alumni data in separate Excel spreadsheets, leading to inefficiencies and limited analytics.

The Challenge

The lack of a centralized data management and analytics platform for tracking, analyzing, and leveraging alumni career paths, engagement, and accomplishments results in three major pain points:

Administrators

Lack timely access to key metrics on employment outcomes, salaries, and industries

  • Comprehensive dashboard for program effectiveness
  • Visual reports generation capabilities
  • Data-based curricular improvements
  • Accreditation and planning support

Alumni

Lack a centralized portal for networking events, career development, and engagement

  • Personalized dashboard for profile management
  • Event registration capabilities
  • Mentorship opportunities
  • Career development resources

Employers

Face cumbersome manual processes to identify and recruit GHU candidates

  • Efficient search and filter by skills, major, GPA
  • Direct candidate communication
  • Pre-qualified candidate pools
  • Streamlined recruitment process

Proposed Solution

A Comprehensive Analytics Platform

The GHU Alumni Connect Analytics Platform is a full-stack web application that centralizes alumni data and generates ongoing insights through intuitive dashboards.

It comprises three integrated portals sharing a common database and user authentication system.

01

Administrator Dashboard

  • Real-time analytics with 6 interactive visualizations
  • Alumni distribution by industry (bar charts)
  • Graduation trends (area charts)
  • Average salary by major (horizontal bar charts)
  • Dropdown filters for major and year range
  • Key metrics: Total alumni, active %, average salary, donations
02

Alumni Portal

  • Personalized dashboard with profile overview
  • Education, current job, engagement metrics
  • Event registration for networking, workshops, reunions
  • Predictions tab with donor likelihood scores
  • Mentor match scores based on engagement history
03

Employer Portal

  • Candidate search with advanced filtering
  • Filter by major, industry, GPA, years of experience
  • Candidate cards with profile summaries
  • Direct "Contact Candidate" functionality
  • Pre-populated message templates

Technical Design

Architecture & Data Design

The platform uses a three-tier architecture: Presentation, Application, and Data layers, designed for scalability, security, and maintainability.

Enterprise Architecture

Three-tier architecture with React SPA, FastAPI backend, and MongoDB database

Presentation Layer

React Single Page Application with responsive design, interactive visualizations, and real-time updates

Application Layer

FastAPI backend providing 15+ RESTful endpoints with automatic documentation and data validation

Data Layer

MongoDB with three main collections: Alumni, Events, and Messages, with indexed fields for performance

Entity Relationship Diagram

Data Design Approach

Synthetic Data Strategy

Due to privacy regulations (FERPA, GDPR), a synthetic-first approach generates data reflecting U.S. higher education patterns without using real personal identifiers.

Data Categories

  • Demographic information
  • Academic history
  • Employment information
  • Skills ratings
  • Engagement behaviors
  • Mentorship characteristics
  • Privacy/consent metadata

Technology Infrastructure

Runs on Kubernetes with nginx ingress routing. Redis caching for optimization. StatefulSets ensure MongoDB data persistence. Horizontal Pod Autoscaling (HPA) provides resilience and scalability.

Lessons Learned & Team Reflection

The project provided significant learning opportunities in teamwork, technical execution, and ethical decision-making.

01

Scope Management

The broad scope required clear role allocation (data engineering, design, analytics, documentation) for parallel collaboration and accountability. Feature prioritization focused on core MVP functionality rather than trying to build everything at once.

02

Regular Check-ins

Daily check-ins and recurring meetings facilitated early identification of blockers and risks, especially during data cleaning phases. This prevented major setbacks and kept the team aligned.

03

Incremental Validation

Emphasized the critical importance of incremental validation for integration usability and pre-agreeing on data standards. This approach reduced rework and improved final product quality.

04

User Experience Focus

Early prototypes highlighted navigation challenges, leading to revisions with clearer labels, simplified workflows, and common layout patterns based on stakeholder feedback.

05

Data Privacy Balance

Negotiating trade-offs between usability, transparency, and legal compliance for granular consent controls taught that privacy involves balancing user autonomy with institutional interests.

06

Embracing Iteration

Almost every project element underwent multiple iterations as understanding deepened. The final product reflects not only technical execution but also a mature collaborative process.

Team Growth & Impact

The project enhanced technical knowledge in data analytics and system design, alongside valuable insights into teamwork, decision-making, and ethical responsibility. Differences in work approaches fostered adaptability through constructive feedback, compromise, and iterative decision-making.

Project Impact & Next Steps

The GHU Alumni Connect Analytics Platform successfully transforms institutional operations from anecdotal to data-driven decision-making, improving fundraising, employer partnerships, career support, and alumni engagement.
